Exotic dance moves

Yes, you are right! As part of my post-doctorate work, I did study exotic tribal dances. To answer your question, my favourite dances are as follows, with definitions:

Polynesian chiken dance
A little known but very influencial dance back as far as 300BC, a whole village would dande around in a circle like chikens, to encourage their livestock or be fruitful.
Icelandic brownian dance
Before the Icelanders developed fire as a means of getting warm, they used to practice this dance, which involved huddling close together in a circle on the floor, and shuffing their bodies around in imperceptably tiny, very rapid movements. One of my fellow students used to say that he thought this dance inspired modem styles such as Tweking.
Artic robot dance
An interesting example of histotic misinformation, English explorers in the 1960s believed to have discovered a new dance style characterised by robotic, stilted movements, being practiced by inhabitants of Northern Alaska and Canada. Later on however it was discovered that they were just moving like this because they were really cold.
